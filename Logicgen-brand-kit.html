§ 00 — Premise

A platform for the next generation of non-technical founders and the AI agents they command — where business logic meets generative velocity, and the first MVP ships before the doubt does.

This document codifies the identity. The mark, the wordmark, the colour, the voice, and the room each requires to breathe. It is the operating manual for everything LogicGen says, shows, and becomes.

§ 01 — Manifesto

We were built for the founder who has the idea, but not the stack — and for the agent that has the stack, but not yet the judgment.

The world is full of brilliant operators — people who have closed deals, run businesses, understood markets — who have been told for thirty years that shipping software is somebody else's job. We disagree.

LogicGen is the platform where business logic, financial models, video understanding, and a thousand other integrations are composable primitives. Where a non-technical founder builds an MVP between meetings. Where an AI agent reaches for advanced tools without permission slips. Where Logic — the structural discipline of a real system — meets Gen — the velocity of a model that ships.

The identity carries that tension by design. A serif that remembers the rigor of an old engineering manual. A sans that runs at the speed of a build pipeline. A single electric pulse that means: this generates.

§ 02 — Pillars 04 / principles

Four loads the identity must carry.

01.

Structural — like code on a clean repo.

Generous margins, monospaced labels, hairline rules. Nothing decorative survives the first edit. Information has a position; the position has a reason.

02.

Velocity — forward, never decorative.

Every visual gesture pushes right, generates, advances. The wedge is not an ornament. It is the chevron of a system in motion.

03.

Editorial — the rigor of a monograph.

One sans family doing all the work. No ornaments, no italics, no swashes — just precision letterforms with generous space. Restraint is the signal that we built a system, not a vibe.

04.

Generative — one bright pulse, never two.

A single colour means this generated. We do not paint surfaces with it. We use it to mark the moment of creation — the cursor, the wedge, the spark.

/note

For the audience — two readers in one room.

The non-technical founder needs reassurance: this is real, this is trustworthy, I can hold this in my hand. The AI agent needs surface area: this is composable, this is structured, this is parseable. The identity is built so both reads land in the same glance.

/with

Built alongside Denova.

Where LogicGen ships your first MVP, Denova powers the integration layer beneath it. The identity flexes to co-brand — the wedge becomes the connector, the L becomes the foundation Denova plugs into.

§ 03 — The Mark primary asset / construction

A foundation, and a wedge — nothing more.

Anatomy

The mark is two components, set on a single black field.

  • A.
    The Foundation A bone-coloured "L" — structural, weight-bearing, unmovable. Logic. The premise the system stands on.
  • B.
    The Wedge A pulse-green hollow chevron. The cursor of generation. Reads simultaneously as code-bracket, play-button, and forward-vector.
  • C.
    The Container A rounded square in ink, with a corner radius of 20% — firm enough to feel engineered, soft enough to feel human.
  • D.
    The Grid Drawn on a 200×200 unit grid. The L occupies cells 50–102×56–144. The wedge occupies cells 112–152×56–144. A 10-unit air gap between them is non-negotiable.

§ 05 — Clear space protection · scale

The mark needs room to do its job.

Rule

Maintain clear space equal to x — where x equals the height of the wedge — on every side of the mark.

Nothing breaches this perimeter. Not type. Not edges. Not other marks. When in doubt, give it more.

Minimum scale

Digital: 24px minimum for the mark, 120px minimum for the horizontal lockup.

§ 08 — Voice how we sound

Speak like a senior engineer who took a writing class.

/01

Confident — never salesy.

We have done the work. We don't need to shout. The shortest true sentence beats the longest persuasive one.

/02

Specific — never vague.

"Ship your MVP in a week" beats "accelerate your journey." Numbers, nouns, verbs, in that order. Marketing-speak is a leak we patch on sight.

/03

Generous — never condescending.

Our reader is a domain expert who happens not to code. We never imply otherwise. The product earns trust by working; the copy earns it by respect.

/04

Editorial — never corporate.

Em-dashes are welcome. Sentence fragments, where they bite. We write like we're explaining the thing to a friend who is smart, busy, and skeptical.

/05

Composable — never monolithic.

Headlines should survive in isolation. Paragraphs should survive without their neighbors. The system, like the product, is built from primitives.

/06

Forward — never apologetic.

We describe what is true now and what we are building next. Hedge words ("hopefully," "ideally," "we believe") are removed on the last pass.

Voice in practice
Do · sounds like LogicGen

Build the financial model. Then route it to Stripe, Notion, and your agent. In an afternoon.

Specific. Concrete. Forward. The em-dash works.

Don't · sounds like a thousand others

Unlock the power of AI to transform your business journey with our cutting-edge platform.

Verbs that mean nothing. Stock phrases. We delete this sentence on sight.
