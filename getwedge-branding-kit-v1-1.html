The anatomy.

The mark is constructed from three geometric beats: the rising left edge, the falling right edge, and the vertical fulcrum line. Built on a 24-unit grid with 2px stroke at base size. Apex sits at unit 12; base spans unit 4 to 20. The fulcrum is the visual hinge — never remove it.

04

The typography.

A simplified two-family system. Inter handles everything from oversized display to small UI labels — the same family at different weights, for a clean and consistent SaaS-platform feel. JetBrains Mono carries any moment that should read as data: timestamps, importance scores, source citations, code. Both are open-source, free, and battle-tested by major SaaS companies.

Inter — Display

Display · Headlines · Hero

The same Inter family used everywhere else, set heavy (700) at oversized sizes for hero moments. Emphasize the key word with the Signal color, not weight or italics — color carries the brand voice.

06

The rules.

A brand becomes itself through what it refuses. These are the choices that will keep Getwedge from drifting into generic SaaS territory as the team grows.

✓

Use Signal color for emphasis

Emphasize the moment that matters with the Signal color — the word, the verb, the wedge. One word per headline, never two. Color does the heavy lifting; weight stays consistent.

✕

Don't bold mid-sentence

Heavy weights (600/700) are reserved for headlines and UI labels. Don't bold individual words in body copy to draw attention — color it Signal green or restructure the sentence instead.

✓

Reserve Signal green for moments

The wedge insight. The CTA. The brand accent. Treat Signal green like a precious resource — used sparingly, it carries weight. Used everywhere, it loses all of it.

✕

Don't use gradient backgrounds

No purple-to-pink. No blue-to-cyan. No "AI shimmer." The brand is built on warm neutrals and one confident color. Atmospheric depth comes from typography and spacing, not effects.

✓

Use the fulcrum line

The vertical line through the wedge is non-negotiable. It's the moment of leverage, the visual hinge. Without it, the mark is just a triangle. Always keep it visible.

✕

Don't rotate or stretch the mark

The wedge always points up. Always. No diagonal versions, no horizontal lay-downs, no perspective transforms. If a layout needs a different orientation, choose a different asset.

✓

Pair display & mono together

The Inter heavy display + JetBrains Mono uppercase kicker is a brand signature. A bold headline with a small all-caps mono label above it is the Getwedge layout move. Use it confidently.

✕

Don't add icons everywhere

Icons should be functional, never decorative. Don't add an icon next to every label, every section header, every nav item. Whitespace and type hierarchy carry the meaning.

07

The voice.

Getwedge sounds like a sharp friend with a strong opinion — never like an enterprise software vendor or a hyped AI startup. We write like we'd talk to a fellow founder over coffee.

How we write

  • Find your wedge.
  • This is what they shipped.
  • Here's why it matters.
  • Three moves to know about.
  • That's your opening.
  • Their customers are complaining about imports.

How we don't write

  • Leverage AI-powered competitive insights.
  • Unlock strategic intelligence at scale.
  • Empower your team with data-driven decisions.
  • Comprehensive analytics for modern businesses.
  • Synergize your competitive positioning.
  • Robust enterprise-grade solutions.
